    Page leans heavily on the "tutorials vs. courses" frame, which is solid. Items the copywriter should consider:

    (1) The "Tutorials teach you syntax. Courses teach you judgement." heading is the strongest line on the page. The two paragraphs below it under-deliver — they describe what new builders get stuck on, but don't connect tightly back to the judgement promise. Worth tightening so the paragraphs earn the heading.

    (2) "The Vibe Code Bible — Build a personal Bible app with AI" is the actual course title. The combination is unusual — "Vibe Code Bible" reads as a tagline, and "Build a personal Bible app" reads as the literal product. Consider whether the page should foreground one or the other. Right now both fight for attention.

    (3) The 3 stat cards (Format / Pricing / Outcome) are functional but the bodies are short almost to the point of feeling unfinished. e.g., "Self-paced modules, web track first." — the "web track first" jargon doesn't help the buyer; it tells them what is NOT yet available.

    (4) The 3 "while you wait" cards are well-structured but the body copy is fine-but-generic. e.g., "Plug the methodology directly into Cursor" — most marketing pages on this site already say this.

    (5) The kickoff CTA at the bottom duplicates the kickoff link in the hero banner. Consider whether double-CTA is intentional or whether one should be a stronger callout.
